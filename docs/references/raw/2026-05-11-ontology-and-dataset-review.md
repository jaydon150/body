### What matters most for your layer-by-layer viewer

The ontologies and the product navigation problem are **not the same problem**. Your viewer wants several simultaneous axes: physical containment, educational system membership, dissection order, and ŌĆ£belongs to multiple systemsŌĆØ navigation. The source data make clear that even anatomy datasets themselves often publish separate **IS-A** and **PART-OF** trees, which is an important warning against using any one source hierarchy as the literal UI tree. ŅłĆciteŅłéturn27view0Ņłéturn27view1Ņłéturn26view0Ņłü

That is why the most robust product architecture is: **UBERON IDs as the canonical node set; app-owned relation tables for `part_of`, `member_of_system`, `dissection_order`, and `display_group`; TA2 labels and synonyms for user-visible naming; FMA crossrefs only where they already exist and help with legacy datasets.** This gives you the graph model you need for structures such as the pancreas and diaphragm without forcing your UX to mirror the limitations or stylistic biases of any single ontology. ŅłĆciteŅłéturn40view0Ņłéturn41search10Ņłéturn10search2Ņłéturn27view0Ņłü

### Standards reality

On the imaging-standard side, the strongest direct standards evidence I found was for **DICOM**, where the standard itself defines anatomy/body-part coding context groups and traces them to SNOMED-derived controlled terminology rather than to FMA, UBERON, or TA2 as native exchange vocabularies. That means ontology choice should not be driven by the hope that FMA or UBERON will drop directly into DICOM. ŅłĆciteŅłéturn41search1Ņłéturn41search13Ņłéturn41search9Ņłü

For **FHIR**, the official public materials gathered here were high-level and did not surface a first-class FMA/UBERON/TA2 anatomy code system in the way DICOM surfaces its own body-part machinery. So the safe conclusion is narrower: **do not choose your backbone ontology expecting ŌĆ£native FHIRŌĆØ payoff**; instead assume you will need a mapping layer to whatever code system your eventual integration guide specifies. ŅłĆciteŅłéturn41search20Ņłéturn41search0Ņłü

### Ontology recommendation

If you need one answer: **commit to UBERON internally, TA2 externally, FMA tactically.** UBERON wins because it is maintained, bridge-oriented, and graph-native enough for a multi-system educational viewer. TA2 should sit on top of it for labels/search because it gives you the strongest human-facing nomenclature story. FMA is still worth preserving in cross-reference tables because too much legacy work and some useful external datasets still point at it, but it is too inactive and too brittle to be the productŌĆÖs center of gravity. ŅłĆciteŅłéturn40view1Ņłéturn10search2Ņłéturn39search0Ņłéturn8search1Ņłü

An alternative can be better under specific conditions. Choose **FMA-first** only if you are building a human-only knowledge system that truly needs the most canonical human anatomical distinctions and you are willing to own cleanup, repairs, and subset curation yourself. Choose **TA2-first** only if this is essentially a nomenclature-first teaching tool and you are comfortable building the real semantics in your own application graph.
